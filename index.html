Story

From Aerodynamics to Coastal Systems

My journey began with aerodynamics and a fascination for the way flow shapes performance, efficiency, and design. What started as an interest in understanding fluid motion gradually became a deeper commitment to solving engineering problems where physics carries real consequence.

That path led me into wind energy, then further into multiphase flow, where modelling became more than a numerical exercise. It became a way to uncover the hidden structure of interacting phases, transport processes, and evolving flow behaviour, then turn that understanding into decisions that matter.

Today, my work sits at the intersection of coastal and offshore engineering, shaped by research and collaborations across the UK and Europe. I am driven by the belief that advanced CFD should do more than describe complexity. It should reveal insight, inspire confidence, and help create systems that are safer, smarter, and more resilient.
